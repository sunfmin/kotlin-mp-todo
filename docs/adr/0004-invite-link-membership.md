# List membership via revocable invite link

A User becomes a member of a List by following an **Invite Link** generated by the List's Owner. The link is multi-use and grants **Editor** on join (there is no viewer role). A List has at most one active link at a time — regenerating revokes the previous one — and the link may carry an optional expiry (default: none). A joiner must be signed in (via OTP) before the link attaches their Editor membership; there is no per-person accept/consent step.

## Why

We chose frictionless "share with my family/team" over per-email invitations with an explicit accept step. A single revocable link is the lowest-friction way to onboard collaborators and composes with the passwordless identity model. Governance is provided by revocation and optional expiry rather than by approving each person.

## Consequences

- Anyone who obtains the link can join as an Editor until it is revoked or expires — the link itself is the access-control boundary, so link leakage equals access leakage.
- The Owner's controls are: regenerate (revokes prior), set/clear expiry, and (out of this decision but implied) remove a member after the fact.
- Per-email invitations and per-person consent are deliberately **not** in scope; they can be added later as a second mechanism without invalidating this one.
